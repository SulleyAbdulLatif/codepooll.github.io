    HTML Entities

    HTML entities are special codes used to display reserved characters and symbols that have special meanings in HTML. Use entities when you want to display characters that are otherwise reserved for HTML markup.

    <p>This is a <strong>bold</strong> statement.</p>
    <p>The copyright symbol is © and the euro currency symbol is €.</p>

    Commonly Used Entities

    Here are some commonly used HTML entities and their corresponding symbols:

    • < - &lt;
    • > - &gt;
    • & - &amp;
    • © - &copy;
    • ® - &reg;
    • € - &euro;

    Special Characters

    Some characters have special meanings in HTML and need to be escaped using entities:

    • < - less than
    • > - greater than
    • & - ampersand

    Unicode Characters

    You can also use Unicode characters directly in your HTML to display a wide range of symbols and characters from different languages and scripts.

    <p>Hello, 😄 This is a smiley face emoji!</p>

    Conclusion

    HTML entities and special characters allow you to display reserved symbols and characters in your web content. Understanding how to use entities and Unicode characters enhances the clarity and expressiveness of your HTML documents.
